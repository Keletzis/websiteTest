AI for Cars and Autonomy


Introduction

Artificial Intelligence is constantly evolving from all aspects. So how can that effect the automotive industry? Autonomy in driving can not only make our roads safer but in addition can also aid the driver in several ways.

However, there are risks. AI in its current state can’t be trusted with human lives inside the automobiles as well as outside of them. In addition, the technical aspect of creating such a program can require many hours of experimentation and trial and error. It requires an incredible amount of technological power and as advanced as it is now it is not perfect with our current technology [Source].

Autonomous Driving


Calculations, methods and advantages

Ou r senses allow us to understand and interact with the world around us. For instance, when we want to cross the street, we use our sight to look for pedestrian crossings, crosswalk markings, other people, and obstacles such as traffic lights. These observations help us determine if it is safe to proceed. Autonomous Vehicles work in a very similar way, as they use cameras, sensors, radars to calculate and measure the distance of an object and react accordingly [Source]. With the use of 3D information, the vehicle at high speeds can swiftly evade an obstacle, much faster than the human reaction time window. AI can access this information with the power of GPUS provided by NVIDIA [3] and open databases such as GoogleNet [4].

In more detail there are two main categories of approaches that use only camera inputs: object-based and pixel-based. Object-based methods are used to identify and classify objects that can be contained within a 2D bounding box within the image. Pixel-based approaches to camera input utilize image segmentation techniques to classify individual pixels, which is particularly useful for identifying objects or features that cannot be enclosed in a bounding box, such as a road surface or lane markings [Source]. Image segmentation involves assigning each pixel in an image to a specific class, such as road, vehicle, pedestrian, tree, building, or sky. This process requires a significant amount of computation and has traditionally been too resource-intensive for real-time applications.

After a vehicle has identified other vehicles and pedestrians in the area and has determined the boundaries of the road and the path ahead, it must decide on the best course of action. This process, called planning, involves several steps: selecting the most efficient route from one location to another while following traffic laws, predicting the likely actions of other parties in the area, and determining the specific actions to take in order to reach the desired location. The vehicle must consider multiple options and select the one that will most effectively and safely bring it to its destination, while also following traffic laws. This decision should be made as efficiently as possible [Source]. All of these statements can be accomplished with the addition of using GPS location and access to the database [Source].

Comfort for the driver


Figure: Comfort in self driving

Considering that we strive for full automation, it no wonder that once we achieve it, the driver will need to be occupied with something. AI can help buy making the user – car experience more accessible with features as well as offer a simpler way for the user to have “fun” inside their automobile until they reach their destination. Almost every technology from modern smartphones has arrived in cars, that includes GPS, Bluetooth and seamless connection between the two is available.





Figure: Tesla Dashboard

Many technologies have advanced over the years as manufacturers can’t go without including a simple music player or in some cases fully functional touchscreen devices that can access the internet. This has opened many doors in the world of infotainment. All inside the car the user can listen to music, search anything online, play games, and everything that the everyday smartphone can do.







Figure: Alexa in Cars

Voice assistant and voice commands can be used to retrieve anything with just the user’s voice. Getting your favorite song, changing the volume, setting the air conditioners temperature are all within reach, all of them just a word away. Gestures are implemented to control the display without even touching it allowing the driver if needed to pay attention to his surroundings.
