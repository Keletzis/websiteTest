How the field of Medicine is changing
in the 4th industrial revolution?

Control Government,progress of technologies,usefulness of techonologies ,Innovations and regulations of them.

Control of Government

1

Governments often control the risks that come with increasing opportunities for new technologies, however, given how rapidly technology evolves, managing new technologies is becoming increasingly complex with current regulatory processes.

Technologies which are progress in 4IR

2

A few examples of new technologies that are thought to increase productivity are artificial intelligence (AI), robots, and 3D printers. This phenomenon has been called the "fourth industrial revolution". These technologies are predicted to replace the current "dominant design" and increase productivity and well-being in the near future.

3

An example of the usefulness of technologies in Medicine

Cyberdyne's HAL is a wearable medical robotic arm that uses advanced technology called cybernics to help move people with physical disabilities. It is difficult for potential consumers to communicate the potential benefits of commercializing this product. However, it must be understood that this is a huge growth in the advancement of Medicine in the future.

Availability of Innovations in different countries

4

Differences in the severity of safety regulations may be the reason for differences in the level of innovation between countries. The case of HAL Cyberdyne, which is often faced by businesses when trying to commercialize an innovation based on new security technology, despite the increasing speed of technological change.

Regulation of new products

5

Because current regulators are often ill-equipped to evaluate a new product, when a company seeks to market its products with innovative technologies, it inevitably faces regulatory hurdles.

Ease of maintaining an appropriate level of control technologies

6

In fact, regulatory barriers prevent many companies from making their creative use of new technologies commercially viable. This is especially true in industries such as health, safety and environmental protection, where regulatory intervention is justified as a way to solve the problem. As a result, it can be quite difficult to establish the proper level of control. In addition, some studies have argued that safety standards can be significantly affected by a country's tolerance for social and cultural risks (Hall and Soskice, 2001; Tate, 2001).
