Images and Graphics Processing


Introduction

Figure: Joel From: The Last of Us 3D Render[Cp]

3D Imaging in all forms is all around us. From the programs available on our smartphones to the games we play on our computers to even the movies that are created, all of them use some sort of 3D environment or similar to showcase a fake world. 2D can also be used effectively to show a more simpler way of looking at an object that doesn’t exist or to immerse the user in a world unknown to them.

Figure: Joel From: The Last of Us 3D Render[Cp]

Of course, it all comes at a cost. There is a great amount of graphical and technical power needed to power all these interfaces and programs. This is where Artificial Intelligence comes in, with its power we can make the algorithms and calculation become almost instant. Many companies have already started developing such programs and are already using them to make the real work needed faster and more efficient.

Real time rendering Using AI

A very important issue is that today’s artists, workers need to be able to see the final product while also working on it simultaneously and unfortunately with today’s hardware that is not yet possible. Artificial Intelligence can cut the rendering times significantly by many different methods. As an early example, creating the huge world of a video game can take a lot of time off the artists hands where it truly doesn’t need to. AI using multiple databases has been able to create fully rendered dungeons filled with objects, creatures and much more, and of course all in minimal time and in the background while the artist focuses on the ideas of implementation [Source].

Figure: DLSS Motion Detection[Cp]

Following the example Artificial intelligence analyses thousands of real-life locations and objects as well as human made objects and determines, as well as creates a fully modeled object from nothing. It applies surface depth, material, roughness to create a believable 3D render[Source]. Special effects in movies are also quite time consuming, using algorithms can help automatically calculate the ways these effects are used in the scene and even entirely create frames between existing ones with no extra effort of the user[Source]. More on how the image generation will is available here. In addition, there are currently available more instant methods of the AI in use such as simple edge enhancing[Source]. This technology will keep advancing in the following years and the algorithm will be able to create even greater worlds and introduce ways helping the user complete tasks faster and more efficiently.

Another great example is NVIDIA’s DLSS technology [Source]. Which takes the game at a lower resolution and upscales it to a higher resolution.
