How the field of education is changing in the
4th industrial revolution?

Is it easy to adapt to new technologies and interact with them?

According to Schwab, the borders between the physical, digital, and biological worlds are fusing during the fourth industrial revolution (4th IR) (2016). Each IR has changed the way we feel, act, and speak. The workers and management must swiftly adapt to this changing environment. They must accept that innovation and risk are unavoidable and be receptive to and prepared for novel ideas. Organizations are unable to compete in this dynamic environment if they lack the requisite skills and the capacity for renewal. Managers must do this type of organizational management if they want their team members to change their long-term outlook, concepts, and attitudes.

This exemplifies how reliance on human assistance is declining as a result of service automation. Inequality arises outside of the realm of technology since connectivity and accessibility are not dispersed fairly. There are 7 billion people on the earth, but only 3.5 billion of them have access to the internet, which causes many to be displaced due to job losses. In 1997, Peter Drucker made a prediction about the future of colleges. Higher education is in terrible shape.

Destiny of colleges in the future

The lifespan of a residential college campus is short. The current (collage) structures are absolutely inappropriate and superfluous. This came to pass when, in 2013, a state-of-the-art, round-the-clock coding university was established in Paris. There are no instructors, textbooks, or tuition expenses. While working on projects, students complete a number of internship programs at various levels. After completing the objectives, they will gain points that will allow them to go on to the next level.

Future instructional strategies will change in a number of ways. the subject matter, lecturers' duties, and students' obligations. It is necessary to reverse the logic of educational systems so that students adapt to the system rather than the other way around. This is at the heart of personalisation.
