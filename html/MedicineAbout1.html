Usage of AI(Artificial Intelligence)
and ML(Machine Learning) in Medicine

Why it is important?

Artificial intelligence technologies are certainly an indispensable help to humanity in the future, however, it is better to understand all the strengths and weaknesses as well as operating with them in order to get the maximum benefit and develop these technologies in the best way.

To strengthen the ACHE competencies for the deployment of corporate AI, the authors recommend that more research be done in the following areas:

  • Modeling and simulation decision support and forecasting
  • Automated data collection
  • Processing robotic process of automatization
  • Smart devices that connect to the facility

Examples of patient self-service.

The researchers suggest that the scientific study of these and other areas will provide sound planning for the implementation of AI in the enterprise such as automating the flow of patients, image analysis ,epidemic simulation and unit transfer forecasting

"AI technology is a double-edged sword with great potential, and threats to medical care.."

The use of corporate technology necessitates the development of new competencies for effective healthcare delivery. Machine learning (ML) applications are used in a variety of medical care scenarios, such as radiography, risk assessment, and precision medicine (Tsay & Patterson, 2018). Healthcare administrators will need to make judgments that have never been made before as demand these and other technology increases. Using the traditional project-based technology deployment that still frequently takes place in the healthcare industry, it is no longer possible to meet the technical requirements for adopting new technologies at the corporate level.

The most creative methods for utilizing AI and machine learning in therapeutic fields have only lately been created (eg, diagnosis and evaluation). Several papers have previously emphasized the potential for usage in administrative healthcare. None of them addressed the organizational execution of the company's competencies and strategy or the underlying scientific principles that underpin how these techniques might influence the support offered.
