Problems and Regulations


Introduction


Considering how Autonomy learns and adapts it is no stranger to errors and issues. Just like humans that are constantly learning Artificial intelligence has made errors that need to be resolved for it to be flawless.

According to data from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, 37,461 people died in car crashes in the United States in 2016. Additionally, 94% of these accidents were caused by human choices or mistakes [Source]. Autonomy’s purpose is to replace driving however these flaws cannot be ignored. Usually, these errors happen because of Sensor failures and algorithmic errors[2]. Of course, AI needs to go through Petabytes of information in order to just function normally. Detecting between all the different objects that could be pedestrians, vehicles and even things like traffic cones makes it really difficult for flawless autonomy to be achieved. As it is in its current state it can’t be fully trusted for full automation. Unfortunately, with our current technology there is no way to predict when these failures will happen and how to prevent them entirely however such as humans can learn the AI can find alternative ways to correct these failures with different sub systems at place[2].

Figure: Flipper Zero[Cp]

Even with the vast amount of data it can collect and interact with, autonomy cant escape from external attacks such as radio frequency attacks and algorithmic jammers[Source] that can either throw the vehicle out of its course or make it directly crash into objects it is supposed to avoid. In addition to that considering almost everything in autonomous vehicles is electronic, outside attacks can mimic the electronic key of the car that can even start it or even affect things inside the car while the passenger – driver is still inside. In general security is always getting better over the years however it can only be so good with the amount of processing power a single vehicle can access at this current age [Source].

Costs


Figure: Money[Cp]

Another important factor is the cost. According to current standards a fully autonomous car can cost from 50000€ all they way up to the hundreds of thousands [Tesla], [Chevy], [Audi] etc. Due to the high prices, they are not available to the majority of people, and if there are no sales companies cannot fully invest in new hardware and personnel that can improve these technologies.

Moreover, the initial costs of accessing this technology is not the only problem. Most if not all autonomous vehicles are electric, which means their batteries will die eventually, needing replacement. And of course any other electronics that might get damaged or, furthermore, the rate at which the technology is improving the parts might even become obsolete and require new ones.

Figure: Electric Car Battery[Cp]

However, as time passes the prices will eventually drop to more accessible levels. Companies will soon overcome many of the troubles caused in the production of vehicles and in addition find ways to cut costs significantly[2].
