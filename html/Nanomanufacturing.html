Nanomanufacturing

Fig. 1 Device for nano-printing electronic chips on large substrates [1].

Nano-manufacturing is manufacturing on a nanoscale. It is achieved through either “bottom-up” or ‘‘top-down” methods in order to construct nanomaterials, devices, structures and systems.

Bottom-up approach

Nanoscale features are being formed with elemental ingredients using the “bottom-up” techniques. Some of those are chemical synthesis, dip-pen lithography, self-assembly, and molecular beam epitaxy[3].

  • “Chemical synthesis is where gaseous precursors and solvents react together to form nanostructures. The nanostructures are then transformed into nanotubes and nanocomposites by using vacuum deposition and nonequilibrium plasma chemistry”[3].
  • “Dip pen lithography is where nanoscale features are produced by tipping an atomic force microscope on a surface”[3].
  • “Self-assembly is the spontaneous cluster of molecules that are building blocks which then form three-dimensional nanostructures”[3].
  • “Molecular beam epitaxy is where thin films on a surface are deposited to a thickness of one atom”[3].

Top-up approach

It is primarily utilised for the production of nano-system components and comprises technologies like lithographic and non-lithographic fabrication[3].

“Lithography is where semiconductor chips, components, and now recently nano components, are made”[3].

Fig. 6 Steps of soft lithography.[6].

Nanomaterials

Nanomaterial is “any material that has an average particle size of between 1 and 100 nanometres”[5]. Due to their small size they posses advantageous in compare to their bigger counterparts. These include strength, toughness, magnetic behaviour and others.

Properties of nanomaterials

Mechanical properties:

  • “They can withstand the extreme strain and they are very strong”[3].

  • Have fewer defects in their structure than regular materials such as greater resistance of fragmentation during bending[3].

Electrical properties:

  • “Nanomaterials electrical properties are dependent on whether the material is metallic or semiconducting”[3].

  • Higher electrical conductivity because of the reduction of structural defects[3].

Thermal Conductivity:

  • Higher thermal conductivity(as much as 10 times higher than that of metals) due to the decrease of structural defects and the vibration of the covalent bonds[3].
Applications of nanomaterials

New applications of nanomaterials are constantly discovered and their number may be infinite. In the following list there are a few examples for different fields.

  1. Energy : “Can be used in a fuel cell, power generation as well as energy saving”[3].

  2. Consumer goods : “can be used in food packaging, as well as for detecting contaminants in food, it can serve well in sporting goods, cosmetic as well as textiles”[3].

  3. Information and Communication Technology : “Can be used in the following areas of information technology, like TV display, optoelectronic devices, as well as memory storage”[3].

  4. Medicine : “Can be used in drug delivery, diagnostic as well as bone regeneration”[3].

  5. Heavy Industry – “Can be used in construction such as coating and glass, it can be used in automotive, catalysis as well as aerospace”[3].
