What is an industrial revolution?

Before we talk about the history of the previous industrial revolutions, we first need to define what an industrial revolution is.

According to the Cambridge dictionary, an industrial revolution is “a period in which the development of machinery leads to major changes in agriculture, industry, transportation and social conditions”[1].


History of the previous industrial revolutions

Figure 1: The three previous industrial revolutions [1]

First industrial revolution

The first industrial revolution ranged from 1760 to 1840 with England being in the centre of this period. The first advancement came from the mechanisation of the textile industry. Such a change played a huge role because of the fact that it changed the way that factories were operating.

Another advancement that led to the first industrial revolution was the invention of the steam engine that made transportation in sea and land through trains and railroads much easier and accessible compared to the past.

All these changes during these years had a vast impact on the population which dramatically increased. However, such changes affected the conditions that women and children were working on to be much more intense and also expanded the gap between the people that were rich with the ones that were poor.

Second industrial revolution

The second industrial revolution occurred between 1870 and 1930. The invention of electricity as well as its application with gas and oil in the industrialization were some of the main reasons that showed a massive rise in the production of the factories at the time. Furthermore, with the revolutionary invention of the telephone in 1877, communications were made more accessible compared to the past.

Figure 3: The invention of Electricity

Figure 4: The invention of telephone

Additionally, significant progress was made in the field of transportation, with the creation of cars in 1886 together with the construction of the first aeroplane in 1903. All these subversive changes led to the expansion of industries and the improvement of the conditions that humans were currently living in.


Third industrial revolution

The third industrial revolution was a revolution that we recently came out of. It originated from 1970 until recently with the origination of the internet and the creation of computers. The use of the internet and computers led to a variety of revolutionary inventions such as the first mobile phone in 1973 as well as the first demonstration of automation in the industry.
